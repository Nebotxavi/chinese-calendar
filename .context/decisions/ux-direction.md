# UX Direction Decision

**Date:** 2026-02-28

## Design Philosophy
Traditional Chinese aesthetics revisited through a modern minimalist lens.

### Core Concept
- **Rooted in tradition** — Chinese calligraphy, red/white palette, decorative motifs, the tear-off calendar metaphor
- **Expressed through minimalism** — cleaner than traditional designs, generous whitespace, restrained use of ornament. Not radical minimalism, but definitively cleaner
- **Inspired by modern China** — the country that actively promotes its ancient traditions while being an ultra-modern, high-tech society. This duality IS the aesthetic: tradition and modernity coexisting naturally, not in tension

### What this means in practice
- Traditional elements (Chinese characters, stamps/seals, decorative borders) are present but refined
- Layout is clean and structured, not dense or cluttered like traditional almanacs
- Typography is deliberate — calligraphic for accent, clean sans-serif for readability
- The app should feel like a beautifully designed physical object, not a data utility
